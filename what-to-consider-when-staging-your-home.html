What to Consider When Staging Your Home

  • Author: OA Real Estate Group
  • Category: Home Listing
  • Date: March 31, 2021

Home staging has one main goal: Get prospective buyers to picture themselves living in the space. The better they visualise the property as their future home, the quicker they will snap it up and close the deal. 83% of buyers' agents in the National Association of Realtors (NAR) 2019's “Profile of Home Staging” report stated that staging enabled them to sell the house faster, and 25% of them said it increased the offer price by up to 5%. So, you get more return for it, and it decreases the duration that property stays on the market. Here are 6 home staging tips to enable you get it right.

Work on the Curb Appeal

Does it say: "Welcome home!" or "Move along, nothing to see here"? Before prospective buyers walk through the front doors, they will have already formed an impression about the house based solely on its curb appeal. Spruce it up, from power washing the walkways and siding, mowing that lawn and tidying up the flowers. Some repainting may be needed, such as for the porch floors that have seen better days.


Focus on Key Areas

Concentrate your efforts in the rooms that will sway the buyer's decision: the living room, kitchen and primary bedroom. Not that you should neglect the rest of the house, but these three key areas will make or break your staging efforts, so you want to bring your A-game here. Set up the furniture in the living room with a neat symmetrical arrangement where it is easy to move around, and pair up the chairs, sofas and lamps to create a conversation area. Aim for a gender-neutral master bedroom complete with clean linen and open up the closets for viewing to show off its storage space. You can even bring your creativity to the kitchen space, such as by setting up a vase with big and bright flowers at the centre.


Go for Neutral Colors

Not everyone is immediately drawn to bright and bold colors. Neutral colors give potential buyers the option to have them or not. You’re not limited to the conventional white or gray here. You can aim for sophisticated neutral colors like the warm greige that's gray with a splash of beige, or the modern mocha that sets a comfortable backdrop.


Let in the Light

Dark rooms cramp up the space. Dimly lit rooms end up feeling tiny. First step: Get as much natural light into the house as possible. Throw back the curtains, open the blinds and clear obstructions that are on the window area. You can also get some table/floor lamps to even out the light from the bulbs and overhead fixtures..


The Mirror Effect

Strategically placing mirrors in the home will add the illusion of space. It ranks up there with large windows and high ceilings. What's more, you can have them as decorative pieces, sprucing up the decor. Even for those rooms without windows, you can set up the mirror on the wall, which will have the effect of adding dimension to the space. Place the mirror facing the window to reflect more light in the room. Just don’t go overboard by having too many mirrors in the house – we don't want you turning it into a carnival funhouse.

Bonus tip: When staging my home, I noticed that pieces of artwork strategically positioned, such as in corners or at the top of the stairs, elicited curiosity, enticing the prospective buyers to check out more of the house. They made for engaging conversations as well – so you can consider it for your property. Remember to stage the outdoors are well, like propping up a nice ace table at the balcony. As you can see, you don’t have to spend much on staging your home. Just a few tweaks will encourage more buyers to make you an offer.
