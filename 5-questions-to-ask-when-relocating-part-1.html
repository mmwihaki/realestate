5 Questions to Ask When Relocating (Part I)

  • Author: OA Real Estate Group
  • Category: Home Relocation
  • Date: April 8, 2021

Should I relocate?

Deciding to move to a new city or home can be life changing. When considering all of your options, it may help to make a list of benefits that your new location will offer, such as better schools, safer neighborhood, cost of living, or better quality of life. These factors can only be decided by you.

If you are moving for a new job, it's more important than ever to find out if your new employer will help cover your expenses. The level of relocation assistance varies widely – with smaller employers less likely to cover it at all. Long-term planning is important since moving is a big commitment and requires a lot of energy, patience, and financial resources.

You know yourself better than anyone else. Consider the idea of not only adjusting to a new job but also a completely new environment.

What’s the best neighborhood for me?

Looking for a new place to live is both exciting and exhausting. Some cities have hundreds of neighborhoods, it can be more than a little overwhelming to pick just one. Many factors will impact where you decide to live.

You might be looking to move into a family-friendly neighborhood with plenty of space for the kids. For parents, the quality of schools in the area is paramount. Researching the crime statistics in the neighbourhood can also be beneficial for safety issues.

Perhaps you're single or a young couple looking for entertainment and nightlife. You will be more interested in the proximity to theatres, concerts, or trendy restaurants. Something to consider will be your commute. If you’re worried about gas prices and traffic, then the public transport in the area matters.


Hidden Costs

When you’re moving, it’s rare that everything goes as planned. These are some hidden costs that you might want to take into consideration.

Moving companies can be overwhelming, with so many options and various contracts. When selecting a company, make sure to get everything in writing. The moving company should list all fees upfront. These are the three most common contracts that you will encounter.

• A non-binding estimate on your contract means the company cannot require payment more than 10% above the original estimate.
• A non-binding to exceed estimate on your contract insures that you will not have to pay for any overages to the original estimate.
• A binding estimate on your contract is supposed to be a guaranteed price for the move and all extras and services.

For Renters, you will need to pay attention to start-up costs such as new utility connections, security deposits, and application fees.

For Buyers, the average closing costs typically range from 2% to 5% of the cost of your home, according to Zillow. These costs include everything from a fee for running your credit report to a loan origination fee, attorney’s fees, the cost of a home inspection, a title search, an appraisal fee, and a survey fee, among other charges. You may get the seller to pay for some or all of these costs, but that typically comes at the expense of a higher sales price on your new home.

You also might need to consider purchasing new furniture because the new space might not be similar to your previous home. Temporary storage will also add up, depending on how long you wait to move into your permanent location.

Does it matter when you move? Summer vs Winter

Proper timing could result into a great deal. Real estate markets in various cities fluctuate seasonally, and picking the right time of the year will have a substantial impact when relocating. Most major cities have more inventory during the summer, but the pricing will increase due to travel, holidays and accessibility. You will most likely find better pricing in the winter months, but due to lack of inventory, it might be more difficult to find the perfect place long term.


Where do I start?

Coordinating all of these moving parts can be stressful but it doesn’t have to be. Selecting the right team to assist with this process will make all the difference. Make sure to do your research and ask plenty of questions before committing to any contracts.
