Wes Aderhold

is a los angeles based artist working primarily in acrylic and oil pastels. He plays with both neutral shapes and figurative forms, all focusing on the underbelly of the human experience.

www.wesaderhold.com

We all have pain, we all have hopes, and we are all striving for connection. I want to create an experience, a story rather, that binds us at the core, for even just a moment. I had to find escapes, as a child, so I used art. I try to get as naked as possible with each piece, let you know you are not alone.

I'm specifically drawn to themes of sexuality, repression, childhood, and the underbelly of human emotion. Life is multifaceted so I'd like for my work to have duality - playfully provocative and dangerously intimate. I just want to leave the world a bit better than I found it.

We invite you to enjoy Wes Aderhold’s two large scale paintings on display at Oxford. If you are interested to know more about him as an artist or want to inquire about purchasing the paintings on site, please visit his website or contact Open Air Homes.

Marco Lorenzetto

Marco Lorenzetto’s artistry is deeply rooted in the history and traditions of his Italian heritage and yet his approach and philosophy are the accumulation of experimentation and exploration.

www.marcolorenzetto.com

A self-taught artist, Marco’s extended travels between Salamanca, Berlin, New York, and Paris exposed him to varied artistic scenes and movements allowing for his own evolution of artistic expression.

Bryce Belyeu

is an artist living and working in New York City. In his work, Belyeu explores the mechanisms of trauma and its relationship to ego development. Through the use of oil, pastel, graphite, and colored pencil, he creates intricate depictions of the mind and its destructive pursuit of equanimity.

www.belyeuart.com

Informed by the Jungian schema of the constellated psyche, Belyeu deconstructs the mind into complexes and associations. These concepts become amorphous collisions of pigment, ensuing networks of ego processes.
