Summer Backyard Ideas: 3 Easy Tips To Improve Your Outdoor Space

  • Author: OA Real Estate Group
  • Category: Home Maintenance
  • Date: April 15, 2021

When the Warmer Months Come Calling

You don't want to be cooped up in your house all day. Sure, the interior design is elegant and comfortable – but you can also style up your outdoor space to make it the ultimate summer hangout. Soak up that summer sun and revel in what nature has to offer right from the safety and serenity of your home. Here are 3 easy ways to prep your backyard as a true R&R sanctuary, creating an enjoyable space for fun and cheer with friends and family.

3 Tips to Prepare Your Backyard for The Summer


1. Prioritise the landscaping

The essence of being outdoors is to take in the fresh air and unwind in a natural setting – so don’t clutter up your yard with loads of furniture. Instead, first focus on the landscaping and florals. For instance, a flower garden will add character to any backyard that it is set up in. In fact, you can go further and take clippings from the garden and spread the same flowers all over the outdoor space.


Lush greenery will make your yard feel organic. Set planters up around the yard, and even allow the foliage to overflow. There are different types of containers that can be used as part of the yard ideas for summer, ranging from the freestanding units to the hanging basket alternatives.

Since you will still need furniture if you’re to relax in your backyard, why not opt for units with natural textures and finishes? It beats cold metal. For instance, you can use rattan chairs which, in addition to complementing the yard, they are also designed to withstand the elements – and that's a priority for your outdoor furniture.

2. Create a focal point

Here, the goal is to centre the design of the backyard around one specific item – which can be anything from a couch or table, a group of plants, a dwarf shrub, or you can go big with a dramatic ornament like a birdbath.

Do you have a large tree close to the garden? It will be a focal point by default, but you can enhance it with a flowering vine. The focal point captures one’s attention, directing the eye and bringing order to the backyard. Large gardens can have multiple focal points, while the smaller ones only need one.

Bonus tip: Gather the family around a fire pit in your backyard for some s'mores and fun stories during those summer nights. This crackling fire pit can be the focal point of the sitting area in the yard, a chill spot after your fun day out in Los Angeles.


3. Divide the Outdoor Space into "Hangout zones"

Creating distinct zones in the backyard will provide different spots for your loved ones to spend time in. These are purposeful zones that appeal to a particular activity as opposed to just having an open space where things are all haywire. You can use flooring materials to separate the different zones, like setting up a lounge area with a concrete flooring, while the rest of the lawn transitions to the pool.


Remember to allow space for walkways. Simply identify the most trafficked areas and place runners there. You can also use potted plants to divvy up the yard, such as separating the outdoor kitchen and dining areas for the OA properties with spacious backyards. Pergolas come in handy for this – designating a "living area" in your yard, and casting just enough shade to allow you to enjoy the outdoors during those hot summer afternoons.
