Santa Barbara Neighborhood Guide

Some of our favorite spots for food, drinks, groceries and activities in Santa Barbara

Restaurants

Finney’s Crafthouse | 35 State St a, Santa Barbara, CA 93101

Family-owned gastropub serving American fare cooked with local, seasonal ingredients.

Bluewater Grill | 15 E Cabrillo Blvd, Santa Barbara, CA 93101

Local chain serving locally and sustainably sourced seafood dishes.

Santa Barbara Shellfish Company | 230 Stearns Wharf, Santa Barbara, CA 93109

Located on Stearn’s Wharf above the water, this restaurant was originally established as a buying station for local shellfish. Now they serve dishes from the local fishermen’s daily catch.

Toma Restaurant & Bar | 324 W Cabrillo Blvd, Santa Barbara, CA 93101

Italian cuisine and wine served with harbor views.

Brophy Bros | 119 Harbor Way, Santa Barbara, CA 93109

Hip, harborside location serving seafood and drinks.

Santo Mezcal | 119 State St, Santa Barbara, CA 93101

Seafood-centric Mexican plates with a modern twist.

Chad’s Cafe | 216 W Cabrillo Blvd, Santa Barbara, CA 93101

Traditional American breakfast and brunch fare served beachside.

The Lark | 131 Anacapa St, Santa Barbara, CA 93101

New American fare cooked with seasonal, locally-sourced ingredients.

Loquita | 202 State St, Santa Barbara, CA 93101

Modern Spanish tapas in a cozy patio setting.

The Drunken Crab | 416 State St, Santa Barbara, CA 93101

Louisiana-style shellfish spiced to order.

Free Things To Do

  • See the Moreton Bay Fig Tree, which is believed to be the largest Ficus macrophylla in the United States, planted in 1876.
  • Take in the salty sea air on Stearns Wharf, a historic pier with dining and shopping.
  • Bike along the coastline or take a walk on the sand at Butterfly Beach.
  • East Beach is a prime beach for tourists on the east side of Stearns Wharf, right by downtown Santa Barbara and hotels.
  • Leadbetter Beach is a sandy, popular beach with light surf, perfect for beginners or windsurfers.
  • Take a nap or have a picnic at Shoreline Park, a grassy oceanside park.
  • Take in the history of Old Mission Santa Barbara, an 18th-century Spanish mission and gardens.
  • Not free, but at a reasonable price of $16 for adults, become immersed in nature along the Santa Barbara Botanic Garden’s quiet trails offering lush views of plants, flowers, and mountains.
  • Alice Keck Park Memorial Garden is another great, free(!) botanical garden park.
  • Have a picnic or barbecue at one of the many tables available around the Rocky Nook Park.
