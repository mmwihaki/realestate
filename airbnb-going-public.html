Airbnb Going Public

  • Author: OA Real Estate Group
  • Category: Investments
  • Date: December 5, 2020

It's finally happening - the long awaited launch of Airbnb on the U.S. stock market. Airbnb has tentatively scheduled its initial public offering for December 9th, 2020, a year that has already witnessed some blockbuster IPOs like record label Warner Music Group, data analytics firm Palantir and data warehouse company Snowflake. As one of the largest and most anticipated U.S. stock market listings, the start-up is expected to trade on Nasdaq under the ticker ABNB, with the intention to sell shares between $44-$50 for the IPO and an expectation of raising $3 billion.



Prior to COVID-19, the company responsible for transforming the travel industry was valued at $31 billion. Unsurprisingly, that number dropped as Airbnb was faced with a decision to prioritize public health over profit. CEO Brian Chesky explored multiple avenues when navigating the best path forward, considering both guests and homeowners throughout the process. Settling on a $215 million dollar payout to hosts to help cover the costs of COVID-19 related cancelations, Airbnb can be accredited for doing the best they could given the circumstances.

Now valued at $18 billion, Airbnb is far from handing the baton to someone else. Despite the downturn, the company has seen a huge recovery as city dwellers flocked to less crowded destinations. The isolation and safety of rental properties was once just a bonus but has shifted to become a requirement for travelers, driving them away from hotels towards platforms like Airbnb. Travel is slowly on the rise yet again and Airbnb is betting that Wall Street will buy into its recovery narrative, making it worth $35 million on a fully diluted basis.

Airbnb going public not only legitimizes short-term rentals for many real estate investors, but spurs interest toward these types of vacation homes as investments. Other factors like record low interest rates, vaccines on the horizon and the big rebound we’ve already seen in this sector undoubtedly have contributed to the uptick being witnessed now. Warm and desirable vacation spots, like LA, San Diego and Palm Springs, are receiving significant attention from investors who are aware that the travel bug is not something that is easily quieted.


2020 has proved to be a pivotal year for the travel industry. New restrictions and detailed guidelines have influenced the very foundation of how and why people travel. These trends are poised to surpass the ramifications of COVID-19, forever changing the travel environments that we've experienced for so long. Airbnb, though hit hard this year, is ready and willing to help orient the future of travel, and their IPO is just another step towards attaining that goal.
